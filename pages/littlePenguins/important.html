Why are Little Penguins So Important?

The Little Penguin is the only species of penguin native to Australia. Although all penguin species are native to the southern hemisphere, only a few are found in cold climates such as Antarctica. In fact, one species, the Galapagos Penguin, is found near the equator.

  • The colony of Little Penguin at Manly has been listed as an endangered population under the Threatened Species Conservation Act in Schedule 1 Part 2 of the Act.
  • The nesting burrows of the Little Penguin colony that was once more extensive is now reduced to only the Manly area.
  • The Scientific Committee of OEH specifies that the population have been reduced to such a critical level that it is in immediate danger of extinction
